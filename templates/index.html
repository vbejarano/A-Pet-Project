Overview

Core Message

Everyone loves pets because they are great companions, they are cute, and they are awesome overall. However, as pet owners and lovers, we should also use the data to determine which pet is best for us. Some pet owners may prefer low-maintence cats, while others enjoy taking care of the more needy cats. The purpose of this project is to share data on the origins and qualities of cats to help the data-driven pet owner find their match.

Core Questions

1. What is the country of origin for the most cat breeds? The least?
2. What is the relationship between a breed's average weight and their average life span?
3. Which cats display the most favorable characteristics?
4. Which cats display the least favorable characteristics?

Breed Origins

We used Leaflet to plot the origins of all of the cats in our database, and Plotly to plot the frequency each country had a cat originate from. Most cats came from the United States (42% of cats), United Kingdom (12% of cats), and Thailand (6% of cats). Fewer cats came from Norway, Somalia, China (all 1.5% of cats).

We found it interesting that the only continent without a native domestic cat species is South America. In addition, there is not a particular connection between the countries with fewer cat origins. One explanation for why the United States has the largest number of cat breeds could be due to the pet industry. Pets are bred for specific characteristics and are most frequently done in the United States.

Weight vs. Life Span

We used Plotly and SQLAlchemy to compare the average of a breed's weight versus their average life span. We found that the Egyptian Mau had the highest average life span of 19 years, while the Aegean had the lowest average life span of 10 years. The Savannah and Ragdoll had the highest average weight of 16 pounds, while the Singapura had the lowest weight of 6 pounds.

Overall, the average life span for most cats tends to be about 14 years. The average weight tends to be between 8 to 12 pounds. There does not appear to be a relationship between the average weight and life span. For example, a higher average weight does not mean that a cat will have a lower average life span and vice versa.

Breeds with the Most Favorable Characteristics

We used Plotly and SQLAlchemy to compare the sum of the following characteristics which we categorized as favorable:

  • Adaptability
  • Child Friendliness
  • Dog Friendliness
  • Affection Level
  • Stranger Friendliness
  • Intelligence
    • Each characteristic is ranked from 1 to 5, 5 being the highest rank in the category. We charted based on the highest scores across these 6 categories.

    We found that the Kurilian had the highest overall sum of these characteristics (30). Most cats which followed tied at 29 overall points for favorable characteristics.

    Breeds with the Least Favorable Characteristics

    We used Plotly and SQLAlchemy to compare the sum of the following characteristics which we categorized as unfavorable:

  • Energy Level
  • Grooming
  • Health Issues
  • Shedding Level
  • Social Needs
  • Vocalisation
    • Each characteristic is ranked from 1 to 5, 5 being the highest rank in the category. We charted based on the highest scores across these 6 categories.

    We found that the Cymric and Balinese had the highest overall sum of these unfavorable Characteristics. Much fewer cats tied in this category, and overall the cats which appeared in this list had a lower overall score than those in the favorable cat list (24 and below).
